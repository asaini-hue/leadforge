FAQ

Questions we always get.

Do I need a Google Tag Manager account already?
No. If you don't have GTM or GA4, LeadForge creates them for you automatically using your Google account. You click "Connect Google account", approve the permissions, and both accounts are created and configured in under a minute — with your service account access already granted.
What if my website is on Wix or Squarespace?
No problem. Wix and Squarespace restrict access to the <head> tag, so LeadForge deploys your lead magnet page to a free subdomain (go.yourdomain.com) on Netlify instead. We configure cross-domain tracking in GTM automatically so your attribution still flows correctly.
Will this replace my existing website or contact page?
Never. LeadForge always creates a new dedicated page alongside your existing site — we never touch or overwrite your current pages. Your contact page stays exactly as it is. The lead magnet page is a new, distraction-free page that you link to from your ads.
How does the Google Sheets CRM work?
When a visitor fills out your lead form, the data is sent to a Google Apps Script webhook that appends a row to your Google Sheet. Each row includes the lead's name, email, phone, city, message, and the full UTM attribution — source, medium, campaign, keyword, and landing page URL. Rows are colour-coded by source so you can scan at a glance. The sheet is yours — no third-party access.
What happens if a lead visits multiple pages before filling out the form?
LeadForge installs a UTM persistence script via GTM on every page of your site. The moment a visitor arrives with UTM parameters in the URL, those values are saved to sessionStorage. Even if they browse three more pages before submitting the form, the original campaign attribution is preserved and written to your CRM.
Is there a long-term contract?
No contracts. Cancel anytime. If you cancel, everything we built is still yours — your GTM container, GA4 property, Google Sheet, and all your lead data. You own all of it. We just won't monitor and maintain it for you anymore.
